Horror Movies

Do you enjoy horror movies? Do they make your skin crawl? If you're not a horror movie fan, you may be puzzled about why people put themselves through the ordeal of watching such movies. Many behavioral researchers question this very fact as well. The claim that people mostly like to experience pleasant emotions and they don't really understand the pleasure in horror movies. Glenn Sparks, a professor, studied the effects of horror films on viewers' physiology. When people watch horrific images, their heartbeat increases,their palms sweat, their skin temperature drops, their muscles tense, and their blood pressure spikes. He noticed a strange pattern when studying horror films: The more fear they felt, the more they claimed to enjoy the movie. And why is this? No one has come to a definite answer. But many suspect that the fear triggers a needed emotion in your brain to produce more healthy emotions. Sparks also noted that people don't necessarily enjoy the movie itself but feelings they have during the movie.
